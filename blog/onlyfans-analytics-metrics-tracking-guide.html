Advanced Analytics Strategies

Strategy #1: Subscriber Segmentation Analysis

Not all subscribers are equal. Segment and analyze separately:

Segment A: Superfans (Top 10% Spenders)

  • Track ARPU, retention rate, custom content purchases
  • What content do they engage with most?
  • What traffic source did they come from?
  • Optimization: Create more content superfans love, acquire more subscribers from their source platforms

Segment B: Regular Buyers (Middle 50%)

  • Occasional PPV purchases, decent engagement
  • What would move them to superfan status?
  • Optimization: Personalized DMs, exclusive offers, custom content suggestions

Segment C: Lurkers (Bottom 40%)

  • Subscribed but minimal engagement, no PPV purchases
  • High churn risk
  • Optimization: Re-engagement campaigns, special discount offers, or accept they'll churn and focus on A/B

Strategy #3: A/B Testing Framework

Test variables systematically to find what maximizes revenue:

What to A/B Test:

  • PPV Pricing: $15 vs. $25 vs. $35 for same video quality
  • Posting Times: Morning vs. evening PPV sends
  • Content Types: Photos vs. videos engagement
  • Subscription Pricing: $9.99 vs. $14.99 vs. $19.99
  • DM Messaging: Casual tone vs. formal tone

A/B Testing Process:

  1. Choose ONE variable to test (don't test multiple simultaneously)
  2. Split audience 50/50 (or test sequentially Week A vs. Week B)
  3. Run test for minimum 2 weeks (need statistical significance)
  4. Measure results (revenue, engagement, conversion rate)
  5. Implement winner permanently
  6. Move to next test

Real A/B Test Example: PPV Timing

Hypothesis: Evening PPV sends convert better than morning

Test:

  • Week 1-2: Send all PPV at 10am
  • Week 3-4: Send all PPV at 8pm

Results:

  • Morning sends: 14.2% avg conversion, $1,680 revenue
  • Evening sends: 26.8% avg conversion, $3,180 revenue

Decision: Permanently shift all PPV to 8-10pm window = +$1,500/month revenue just from timing change

Common Analytics Mistakes

Mistake #1: Vanity Metrics Over Revenue Metrics

Problem: Focusing on follower counts and likes instead of revenue-driving metrics

Fix: Track "so what?" for each metric. "10k Instagram followers" = vanity. "10k IG followers converting at 2% = 200 subs/month" = actionable.

Mistake #2: Not Tracking Long Enough

Problem: Making decisions based on 1-2 weeks of data (too small sample size)

Fix: Minimum 30 days of data before drawing conclusions. 60-90 days better for trend analysis.

Mistake #3: Analysis Paralysis

Problem: Tracking 50 metrics, spending 5 hours weekly analyzing, never taking action

Fix: Track the essential 15 metrics. Spend 30 minutes weekly reviewing. Spend rest of time executing based on insights.

Mistake #4: Ignoring Qualitative Data

Problem: Only looking at numbers, not reading subscriber feedback/DMs

Fix: Balance quantitative (numbers) with qualitative (what subscribers actually say). Monthly review of DM themes and feedback.
