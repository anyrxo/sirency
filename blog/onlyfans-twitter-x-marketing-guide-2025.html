Avoiding Twitter Shadowbans: The Complete Guide

A shadowban is when your content is hidden from non-followers, devastating your growth potential. Here's how to avoid it:

What Causes Twitter Shadowbans

  • Overuse of hashtags: Especially banned or spammy ones
  • Too many duplicate tweets or links: Looks like spam
  • Rapid following/unfollowing: Signals bot behavior
  • Excessive @mentions: Appears as spam tagging
  • High user report rate: Competitors or trolls mass-reporting
  • Posting exact same content repeatedly: Copy-paste tweets

Shadowban Prevention Strategies

1. Vary Your Content

Never post identical tweets. Even when promoting the same OnlyFans content, vary the caption:

  • Tweet 1: "New photoshoot just dropped on OnlyFans üî•"
  • Tweet 2: "You're going to love what I just posted... check my OF üíï"
  • Tweet 3: "100+ new photos live now. Link in bio üì∏"

2. Limit Link Posting

Don't include your OnlyFans link in every tweet. Twitter flags accounts that spam links:

  • Include link in 1 out of every 3-4 tweets
  • Other tweets: "Link in bio" (drives profile visits)
  • Use Twitter's native link shortener occasionally

3. Gradual Growth, Not Explosive

Sudden follower spikes trigger algorithmic review:

  • Avoid "follow for follow" schemes
  • Don't buy followers (obvious bot accounts get you banned)
  • Grow steadily through authentic engagement

4. Engage Authentically

Show Twitter you're a real person, not a bot:

  • Reply to comments on your tweets
  • Like and retweet other creators' content
  • Participate in conversations beyond self-promotion
  • Use varied language (not robotic responses)

5. Check for Shadowban Regularly

Use shadowban checker tools:

  • Search your @username while logged out‚Äîdo your tweets appear?
  • Search your recent hashtags‚Äîdo your tweets show up?
  • Use third-party checkers (shadowban.eu, hisubway.online)

What to Do If You Get Shadowbanned

  1. Stop all automated activity immediately (bots, schedulers)
  2. Review recent tweets‚Äîdelete any that violated guidelines
  3. Take a 48-hour posting break
  4. Resume with 100% organic, varied content
  5. Appeal to Twitter Support if shadowban persists beyond 7 days
