üìÖ The 4-Day Content Sprint Method

The 4-Day Content Sprint is the workflow system used by professional OnlyFans promotion agencies to create 30 days of content in 4 focused sessions.

4-Day Sprint Structure

Day 1: Planning & Preparation (2-3 hours)

Goal: Plan entire month's content strategy before filming anything

  • Content calendar creation:
    • Map out 30 days of feed content (3-5 posts daily = 90-150 posts)
    • Plan PPV drops (4-6 per week = 16-24 PPVs)
    • Schedule Stories themes/topics (8-15 daily = 240-450 Stories)
    • Note special events, holidays, or promotions
  • Shot list creation:
    • List every piece of content you need to film
    • Group by location, outfit, and setup
    • Prioritize high-value PPV content
  • Outfit & prop preparation:
    • Lay out all outfits for month (12-20 outfits)
    • Gather props, toys, accessories
    • Prep backup outfits for variety

Day 2: Feed Content Batch Filming (6-8 hours)

Goal: Film all feed posts, Stories, and low-tier PPV content

  • Setup phase (30 min):
    • Set up 2-3 camera angles simultaneously
    • Test lighting in all filming locations
    • Prepare all outfits in order of shooting
  • Filming phase (4-5 hours):
    • Film in "outfit blocks" (all content in Outfit 1, then switch to Outfit 2, etc.)
    • Shoot 3-5 variations of each pose/scenario
    • Film 90-120 pieces of feed content
    • Capture 50+ clips for Stories
  • Review phase (1 hour):
    • Quick review of footage to ensure quality
    • Note any reshoots needed
    • Organize files into folders

Day 3: Premium PPV Content Batch Filming (6-8 hours)

Goal: Film all high-value PPV content, customs, and exclusive videos

  • High-production PPV (4-5 hours):
    • Film 8-12 full-length PPV videos (8-15 min each)
    • Multiple angles, scenarios, and variations
    • Capture behind-the-scenes footage for bonuses
  • Custom content templates (1-2 hours):
    • Film "fill-in-the-blank" custom videos (you can personalize later)
    • Create custom photo sets with multiple scenarios
    • Pre-film common custom requests

Day 4: Editing & Scheduling (6-8 hours)

Goal: Edit all content and schedule entire month

  • Batch editing (4-5 hours):
    • Edit all PPV videos using templates/presets
    • Batch edit feed photos (apply same filters/adjustments)
    • Create GIF previews for PPV content
    • Render all content overnight if needed
  • Scheduling & captions (2-3 hours):
    • Schedule all feed posts for 30 days
    • Pre-write captions using templates
    • Schedule PPV messages with previews
    • Queue Stories content

Total time investment: 20-27 hours over 4 days = 30 days of content + freed-up time for growth activities

üé¨ Batching Strategy Breakdowns

1. "Outfit Block" Filming Strategy

The Outfit Block Method is used by OnlyFans agencies to minimize setup time and maximize efficiency.

How It Works:

Instead of: Filming one piece of content ‚Üí changing outfit ‚Üí filming another ‚Üí changing outfit (massive time waste)

Do this: Film ALL content planned for Outfit #1 in one continuous session, then move to Outfit #2

Example Outfit Block:

  • Outfit 1: Red lingerie set
    • Feed post #1: Bedroom mirror selfie (variations: sitting, standing, lying)
    • Feed post #2: Close-up tease
    • Feed post #3: Full-body pose
    • PPV #1: 10-minute red lingerie tease video
    • Stories content: 5 quick clips (trying on, teasing, BTS)
    • Total filmed in 45 minutes: 10 pieces of content
  • Outfit 2: Casual homewear
    • Feed posts: 3 "girl next door" vibe photos
    • PPV #2: "Just woke up" intimate video
    • Stories: 4 "morning routine" clips
    • Total filmed in 40 minutes: 8 pieces of content

Efficiency gain: 18 pieces of content in 85 minutes vs. 4-5 hours if filmed individually

2. "Multi-Angle Filming" Strategy

Film with 2-3 camera angles simultaneously to create multiple pieces of content from one session.

Setup:

  • Camera 1: Tripod at eye level (main angle, full-body shots)
  • Camera 2: Low angle or side angle (alternative perspective)
  • Camera 3 (optional): Close-up/detail shots (phone on stand or second tripod)

Result:

One 10-minute filming session = 3 different videos from 3 angles = 3 separate PPVs or variations

Example:

  • PPV #1: "Full experience" video (Camera 1 main angle) - $27
  • PPV #2: "Alternate angle exclusive" (Camera 2 side angle) - $15
  • PPV #3: "Close-up version" (Camera 3 detail shots) - $12

Revenue impact: 10 minutes filming = $54 potential revenue vs. $27 with single angle (2x revenue, same time investment)

3. "Template & Variation" Strategy

Create content templates that can be quickly varied for different posts.

Example: Photo Set Template

Base setup: Bedroom, specific lighting, camera angle

Variations (film all in one session):

  1. Variation 1: Lingerie set A ‚Üí 10 photos
  2. Variation 2: Lingerie set B (just outfit change) ‚Üí 10 photos
  3. Variation 3: Lingerie set C ‚Üí 10 photos
  4. Variation 4: Bodysuit ‚Üí 10 photos
  5. Variation 5: Topless/nude (same poses as above) ‚Üí 15 photos

Total: 55 photos in 90 minutes = enough feed content for 18+ days (if posting 3x daily)

Key insight: Once setup is perfect, changing ONLY the outfit creates variety without resetting camera/lighting

üìä Advanced Batching Tactics

1. The "Evergreen Content Bank" System

Build a library of evergreen content that can be scheduled/recycled indefinitely.

How to Build Your Evergreen Bank:

  • Concept: Film 50-100 pieces of timeless content (no seasonal/dated references) that can be posted repeatedly
  • Content types:
    • Classic poses/angles that always perform well
    • Lingerie sets in neutral colors (black, white, red)
    • Shower/bathroom content (always relevant)
    • Bedroom mirror selfies
    • "Good morning" and "goodnight" photos
  • Usage: Rotate these into your schedule when you're short on new content or need to fill gaps
  • Recycling rule: Wait 60-90 days before reposting same evergreen content (most subscribers won't remember)

Result: Never scramble for content‚Äîyou always have a backup bank to pull from

2. The "Content Repurposing Pyramid"

Create one high-value piece of content, then repurpose it into 10+ smaller pieces.

Repurposing Example:

Base content: 15-minute premium PPV video

Repurposed into:

  1. PPV #1: Full 15-min video ($35)
  2. PPV #2: "First 5 minutes" teaser version ($12)
  3. Feed post #1: Best screenshot from video
  4. Feed post #2: Different angle screenshot
  5. Feed post #3: Behind-the-scenes setup shot
  6. Stories content #1-5: 5 short clips (5-10 sec each) teasing full video
  7. Twitter promo: SFW teaser clip with OnlyFans link
  8. Reddit post #1: SFW screenshot to drive traffic
  9. Reddit post #2: GIF preview with "full video on OF" caption
  10. Instagram Reels: Blurred/censored teaser
  11. Custom content template: Re-edit video with personalized intro for customs

Total: 1 filming session = 15+ pieces of content across all platforms

3. The "Theme Week" Batching Strategy

Film content in weekly themes to simplify planning and create narrative continuity.

Example Theme Week Schedule:

  • Week 1 Theme: "Red Hot" ‚Üí All red lingerie, outfits, lighting
  • Week 2 Theme: "Cozy Homebody" ‚Üí Sweatshirts, casual, "girl next door" vibe
  • Week 3 Theme: "Shower Time" ‚Üí All bathroom/shower content
  • Week 4 Theme: "Fantasy Roleplay" ‚Üí Cosplay, scenarios, themed content

Benefits:

  • Easier planning (one theme = clear creative direction)
  • Faster filming (all content uses similar setups/outfits)
  • Subscribers look forward to weekly themes (engagement boost)
  • Easier to create cohesive PPV promotions ("This week's Red Hot collection")

üí° Common Batching Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake #1: Batching Without a Content Calendar

The problem: Filming tons of content but no plan for when/how to use it.

The fix: ALWAYS plan your content calendar BEFORE filming. Shot list must align with scheduled posting dates.

‚ùå Mistake #2: Trying to Batch Everything in One Day

The problem: 16-hour filming marathon leads to exhaustion, declining quality, and burnout.

The fix: Use 4-Day Sprint method. Break batching into manageable sessions across multiple days.

‚ùå Mistake #3: Not Building a Content Buffer

The problem: Batching exactly 30 days of content with zero buffer = one missed session derails everything.

The fix: Always maintain a 7-14 day content buffer. If you batch 30 days, schedule only 20-25 and keep rest as backup.

‚ùå Mistake #4: Sacrificing Quality for Quantity

The problem: Rushing through batching results in low-quality content that performs poorly.

The fix: Batching is about EFFICIENCY, not rushing. Take time to nail lighting, angles, and quality‚Äîjust do it all in one session.
