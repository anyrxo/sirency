1. Commission Structure & Payment Terms

What to look for: This clause defines how much the agency earns and when/how you get paid.

Key questions this clause must answer:

  • Commission percentage: What percentage of gross earnings does the agency take? (Standard: 20-40%)
  • Calculation method: Is commission based on gross revenue (all earnings) or net revenue (after OnlyFans' 20% cut)?
  • Payment schedule: How often are you paid? (Monthly, biweekly, weekly?)
  • Revenue scope: Does commission apply to ALL revenue streams (subscriptions + PPV + tips + custom content) or only certain streams?
  • Additional fees: Are there any fees beyond commission? (Payment processing, software, tools?)

‚úÖ Fair commission terms:

  • 20-40% flat commission based on gross monthly OnlyFans earnings
  • OnlyFans pays you directly (agency NEVER touches your money)
  • You invoice/pay agency monthly based on earnings reports
  • No hidden fees beyond stated commission percentage
  • Clear definition: "Gross earnings means all revenue received from OnlyFans platform including subscriptions, PPV, tips, and messages"

üö© Predatory commission terms:

  • 50%+ commission (excessive‚Äîno agency justifies this)
  • Commission calculated on NET earnings after taxes/expenses (gives agency incentive to inflate "expenses")
  • Agency receives payments directly from OnlyFans (major red flag‚Äîthey control your money)
  • Vague language: "Commission based on revenue as determined by agency" (no specific percentage)
  • Additional fees: "$500 monthly software fee + 30% commission" (double-dipping)

2. Services Provided (Scope of Work)

What to look for: Detailed description of exactly what services the OnlyFans agency will provide for their commission.

Must be specifically defined:

  • Social media management: Which platforms? How many posts per week per platform?
  • Fan chat management: 24/7 coverage? Response time SLA? How many chatters assigned?
  • Content strategy: Weekly planning calls? Monthly content calendars?
  • Analytics: What metrics tracked? How often are reports provided?
  • DMCA protection: How many sites monitored? How often? Guaranteed takedown response time?
  • Paid advertising: Included or additional cost? Budget requirements?

‚úÖ Clear service terms:

  • Detailed service list with specific deliverables: "Instagram (15 posts/week), Twitter (20 posts/week), Reddit (10 posts/week)"
  • "24/7 fan chat management with 90% response rate within 15 minutes"
  • "Weekly strategy calls every Tuesday at [time]"
  • "Monthly analytics reports delivered by the 5th of each month"
  • "DMCA monitoring of 25+ piracy sites with 24-48 hour takedown filing"

üö© Vague service terms:

  • "Full social media management" (which platforms? How many posts?)
  • "Chat support when available" (not 24/7? Random hours?)
  • "Regular strategy sessions" (how often is "regular"?)
  • "We'll help with content" (what does "help" mean specifically?)
  • "DMCA services as needed" (how often? Which sites?)

3. Contract Duration & Termination Rights

What to look for: How long you're committed and how/when you can end the relationship.

Critical termination questions:

  • Initial term length: How long is the first commitment? (3-6 months is standard)
  • Auto-renewal: Does the contract automatically renew? For how long?
  • Cancellation notice: How much advance notice required to cancel? (30-60 days is fair)
  • Cancellation penalties: Are there fees for early termination?
  • Performance clauses: Can you terminate if agency doesn't deliver results?
  • For-cause termination: Can you terminate immediately if agency breaches contract?

‚úÖ Fair termination terms:

  • "Initial 3-month term, then month-to-month"
  • "Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice after initial term"
  • "No cancellation fees after initial 3-month term"
  • "Creator may terminate immediately for cause if agency fails to provide agreed services for 14+ consecutive days"
  • "Performance guarantee: If revenue doesn't increase by 30% within 6 months, creator may terminate without penalty"

üö© Predatory termination terms:

  • "24-month minimum term, non-cancellable" (trap contract)
  • "$15,000 cancellation fee if terminated before 12 months" (punitive)
  • "Automatically renews for another 12 months unless canceled 90 days in advance" (sneaky renewal)
  • "Agency may terminate at any time, but creator cannot terminate for 18 months" (one-sided)
  • "All termination requests subject to agency approval" (they control your exit)

4. Content & Subscriber Ownership Rights

What to look for: Who owns your content, subscriber relationships, and social media accounts.

Critical ownership questions:

  • Content ownership: Do you retain full ownership of all content you create?
  • Subscriber ownership: Do you own subscriber relationships acquired during the contract?
  • Social media accounts: Who owns Instagram/Twitter/TikTok accounts created by the agency?
  • Post-termination rights: Can you keep subscribers/accounts after leaving the agency?
  • Content usage: Can the agency use your content for marketing after termination?

‚úÖ Creator-protective ownership terms:

  • "Creator retains 100% ownership of all content created, including photos, videos, and written material"
  • "Creator owns all subscriber relationships. Agency has no claim to subscribers upon termination"
  • "Social media accounts created during contract remain creator's property"
  • "Upon termination, agency must transfer all account access within 7 days and cease using creator's content"
  • "Agency may use creator content for marketing portfolio only with ongoing written consent"

üö© Predatory ownership terms:

  • "Agency owns 50% of all subscribers acquired during contract period" (stealing your audience)
  • "Creator grants agency perpetual license to use all content for any purpose" (they can use your content forever)
  • "Social media accounts remain agency property upon termination" (you lose accounts YOU built)
  • "Agency retains commission rights on all subscribers for 24 months post-termination" (ongoing commission drain)
  • "Creator may not contact subscribers for 12 months after leaving agency" (non-compete preventing you from keeping your fans)

5. Performance Expectations & Guarantees

What to look for: What results can you realistically expect, and what happens if agency underperforms?

Performance questions:

  • Earnings projections: What revenue growth does the agency project? (Realistic vs. guaranteed)
  • Timeline: How long until you see results? (4-8 weeks minimum for any legitimate strategy)
  • Performance metrics: How will success be measured? (Revenue growth, subscriber count, engagement?)
  • Underperformance remedies: What happens if results don't materialize?
  • Refund policies: Are there any performance-based refunds?

‚úÖ Realistic performance terms:

  • "Agency projects 3-8x revenue growth over 6-12 months based on historical data, though individual results vary"
  • "Initial results typically visible within 4-8 weeks, significant growth within 3-6 months"
  • "If revenue doesn't increase by minimum 30% within first 6 months, creator may terminate without penalty"
  • "Success measured by: monthly revenue growth, subscriber retention rate, engagement metrics"
  • "No guaranteed specific earnings‚Äîresults depend on creator consistency, niche, and market factors"

üö© Unrealistic performance terms:

  • "Guaranteed $50,000 monthly within 90 days" (impossible to guarantee)
  • "You'll be top 0.1% within 6 months or your money back" (unrealistic promise)
  • "No performance metrics defined‚Äîtrust us, you'll make money" (vague, unaccountable)
  • "Results are not guaranteed, and agency bears no responsibility for earnings" (taking commission with zero accountability)

6. Creator Responsibilities & Requirements

What to look for: What are YOU required to provide/do for the agency to fulfill their services?

Common creator obligations:

  • Content creation volume: Minimum posts per week/month? (15-30 pieces weekly is typical)
  • Content delivery schedule: When must content be provided to agency for scheduling?
  • Communication requirements: Weekly calls? Response time to agency messages?
  • Brand compliance: Guidelines for content type, messaging, brand image?
  • Exclusivity: Can you work with other agencies or platforms simultaneously?

‚úÖ Reasonable creator obligations:

  • "Creator agrees to produce minimum 15-20 pieces of content weekly (photos/videos)"
  • "Creator will provide content to agency by Friday each week for following week's scheduling"
  • "Creator will participate in 30-minute weekly strategy call every [day/time]"
  • "Creator will respond to agency communications within 48 business hours"
  • "Creator may work with other platforms (Fansly, Patreon) but not other OnlyFans management agencies during contract term"

üö© Unreasonable creator obligations:

  • "Creator must produce 100+ posts weekly" (unsustainable, unrealistic)
  • "Creator must be available for calls 24/7 at agency's request" (unreasonable availability demands)
  • "Creator cannot work with any other platforms or revenue streams" (controlling your entire income)
  • "Creator must follow all agency directives without question" (removing your creative control)
  • "Creator must provide agency with passwords to all personal accounts" (security risk)

7. Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure

What to look for: Protection of your personal information and business data.

Confidentiality provisions should cover:

  • Agency confidentiality: Agency cannot disclose your real identity, earnings, or business details without permission
  • Mutual NDA: Both parties agree not to share confidential information
  • Data protection: How agency handles/stores your personal data and content
  • Testimonial rights: Agency can only use your testimonial/case study with explicit written consent

‚úÖ Strong confidentiality terms:

  • "Agency agrees to maintain strict confidentiality of creator's real identity, earnings data, and personal information"
  • "Agency may not use creator's name, image, or earnings for marketing without advance written consent"
  • "All creator data stored with enterprise-grade encryption and deleted within 30 days of contract termination"
  • "Mutual confidentiality: Both parties agree not to disclose business strategies, financial data, or confidential information"

üö© Weak/one-sided confidentiality terms:

  • "Creator agrees to keep agency methods confidential, but agency may use creator data for marketing at any time" (one-sided)
  • "Agency owns all data collected and may use for any purpose" (your data exploited)
  • "No confidentiality provisions" (major red flag‚Äîyour data is unprotected)

8. Dispute Resolution & Legal Jurisdiction

What to look for: How disputes are handled if problems arise.

Dispute resolution options:

  • Mediation: Third-party mediator helps resolve disputes (less expensive than court)
  • Arbitration: Binding arbitration instead of court litigation
  • Jurisdiction: Which state/country's laws govern the contract?
  • Attorney fees: Who pays legal fees if dispute goes to court?

‚úÖ Fair dispute resolution:

  • "Disputes will first be addressed through good-faith mediation. If unresolved, either party may pursue legal action"
  • "Contract governed by laws of [your state], and any legal action must be filed in [your state] courts" (convenient for you)
  • "Prevailing party in legal dispute entitled to reasonable attorney fees" (both sides have skin in the game)

üö© Unfair dispute resolution:

  • "All disputes subject to binding arbitration in [agency's home country]" (expensive, inconvenient for you)
  • "Creator waives right to jury trial and class action participation" (limiting your legal options)
  • "Creator responsible for all legal fees regardless of outcome" (discourages you from pursuing valid claims)

9. Indemnification & Liability Limits

What to look for: Who is responsible if legal issues arise (copyright claims, platform bans, etc.).

Indemnification questions:

  • Platform bans: Is agency liable if their strategies cause your OnlyFans account to be banned?
  • Copyright issues: Who is responsible if agency-created content infringes copyright?
  • Liability caps: Is agency's financial liability limited?
  • Creator protection: Are you protected from agency's errors/negligence?

‚úÖ Balanced indemnification:

  • "Agency indemnifies creator against claims arising from agency's negligence or violation of platform terms"
  • "Creator indemnifies agency against claims arising from creator's content or platform violations"
  • "Agency's liability capped at total fees paid by creator in preceding 3 months" (reasonable limit)

üö© One-sided indemnification:

  • "Creator agrees to indemnify agency against all claims, but agency bears no responsibility for any outcomes" (you're liable for everything, they're liable for nothing)
  • "Agency's total liability limited to $100 regardless of damages" (meaningless protection)
  • "Creator waives all claims against agency for any reason" (zero agency accountability)

10. Amendment & Modification Rights

What to look for: Can the agency change contract terms mid-agreement?

‚úÖ Fair modification terms:

üö© Unfair modification terms:

  • "Continued use of services constitutes acceptance of changes" (forced acceptance)
