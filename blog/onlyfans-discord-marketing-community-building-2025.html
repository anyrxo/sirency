Common Discord Marketing Mistakes to Avoid

1. Creating a Server But Never Being Active

A dead Discord is worse than no Discord at all. If you can't commit to daily activity, don't create a server yet. Dead servers make you look unprofessional and hurt your brand.

2. Making Everything Subscriber-Only

You need free-access channels with value to attract non-subscribers. Use the 70/30 rule: 70% accessible to everyone, 30% locked behind subscription. Free members need to see the value before paying.

3. Ignoring Moderation

Unmoderated servers quickly become toxic. Trolls, spammers, and inappropriate behavior will drive away your real fans. Invest in moderation from day one.

4. Over-Promoting, Under-Engaging

If every message is "Subscribe to my OnlyFans," members will leave. Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% genuine engagement and value, 20% direct promotion.

5. Not Utilizing Discord Analytics

Discord provides Server Insights showing growth, active members, and engagement trends. Review monthly to identify what's working and what needs adjustment.
