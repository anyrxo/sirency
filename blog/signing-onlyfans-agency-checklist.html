What to Look for When Signing with an OnlyFans Agency: The 15-Point Checklist

Published November 11, 2024 · 15 min read
Back to Blog

Signing with the right OnlyFans agency can 10x your earnings. Signing with the wrong one can cost you $50k+ in lost revenue and trap you in a predatory contract for years.

After reviewing 200+ agency contracts and helping creators escape exploitative agreements, we've identified the exact 15 criteria that separate world-class agencies from frauds, amateurs, and everyone in between.

This is your comprehensive, actionable checklist. Print it. Reference it during negotiations. Don't sign until every single item checks out.

The 15-Point Agency Evaluation Checklist

1. Commission Structure & Fee Transparency

What to Look For:

  • Clear percentage (typically 25-40%) with exact calculation methodology
  • Commission calculated AFTER OnlyFans' 20% platform fee, not before
  • Zero hidden fees (no "technology fees," "platform fees," or "processing charges")
  • Written examples showing how commission is calculated on different revenue scenarios

Red Flags:

  • Vague language like "competitive rates" or "industry standard commission"
  • Commission on gross revenue (before OnlyFans takes their cut)
  • Additional fees beyond base commission
  • Sliding scale commission that increases as you earn more (should decrease or stay flat)

Ideal Scenario: "Agency receives 30% of Net Creator Earnings, calculated as: (Total OnlyFans Gross Revenue - OnlyFans 20% Fee) × 30% = Agency Commission"

2. Contract Duration & Termination Terms

What to Look For:

  • Initial term of 6-12 months maximum for new partnerships
  • 30-90 day termination notice requirement (reasonable for both parties)
  • Termination fee capped at 1-2 months average earnings (if any)
  • Performance-based termination clause (e.g., "creator may terminate without penalty if earnings don't increase X% within 90 days")

Red Flags:

  • Contracts longer than 12 months for first-time partnerships
  • Termination fees exceeding $5,000 or 3+ months of earnings
  • Post-termination commission (agency continues earning % after you leave)
  • Automatic renewal without explicit re-agreement

Questions to Ask: "What happens if I want to terminate in month 3? What exactly would I owe? Can you show me the calculation in writing?"

3. Detailed Service Scope & Deliverables

What to Look For:

  • Specific list of services with frequency (e.g., "24/7 DM management via dedicated 3-person chat team")
  • Marketing channels and activity levels (e.g., "Daily posts on 15 targeted subreddits, 5 tweets/day, IG story promotion 3x/week")
  • Content strategy support (calendar planning, niche optimization, PPV pricing strategy)
  • Analytics and reporting cadence (weekly/monthly performance reports with actionable insights)
  • Point of contact and communication protocol

Red Flags:

  • Vague promises like "full account management" or "complete growth optimization"
  • No specifics on WHO will be doing the work or HOW OFTEN
  • Services described in marketing speak rather than concrete deliverables

Must-Have Document: Detailed "Scope of Services" addendum listing every deliverable with frequency, responsible team member, and success metrics

4. Chatting Team Structure & Quality Control

What to Look For:

  • Human chatters (NOT bots or automation software)
  • Dedicated team assignment (not shared across dozens of creators)
  • Training process for maintaining your voice/personality
  • Quality control measures and creator approval processes
  • Ability to review chatter conversations and provide feedback

Red Flags:

  • AI/bot-based messaging (violates OnlyFans TOS and destroys engagement)
  • Offshore outsourced chatters with no training
  • No creator oversight of chat conversations
  • One chatter managing 20+ creator accounts

Questions to Ask: "Can I meet the chatters who'll work on my account? How do you train them to match my personality? What oversight do I have?"

5. Account Access & Security Protocols

What to Look For:

  • Zero requirement for your OnlyFans login password
  • Use of OnlyFans' official Agency/Manager features (if available)
  • Read-only analytics access or screen-sharing for collaboration
  • You maintain 100% control of account credentials and payout settings

Red Flags:

  • Demands full login credentials
  • Requires being added as account admin with withdrawal permissions
  • Wants access to your bank account or payment information

Non-Negotiable: You should NEVER give an agency your password. If they insist, walk away immediately. This is a scam.

6. Performance Metrics & Accountability

What to Look For:

  • Defined KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) they'll track and improve
  • Regular reporting schedule (weekly or monthly) with specific metrics
  • Realistic growth projections based on your current baseline
  • Performance review meetings to discuss results and strategy adjustments

Key Metrics to Track Together:

  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) growth
  • Subscriber acquisition cost (SAC)
  • Average revenue per user (ARPU)
  • Subscriber churn rate
  • PPV conversion rates
  • Free trial to paid conversion rates

Red Flags:

  • No performance tracking or reporting
  • Refusal to share analytics or growth data
  • Guaranteed specific dollar amounts ("we guarantee $10k/month!")

7. Client References & Verifiable Case Studies

What to Look For:

  • Minimum 3-5 current creator references you can speak with directly
  • Case studies with before/after metrics (can be anonymized)
  • Video testimonials from real creators (not stock footage/AI generated)
  • Verifiable proof of results (screenshots of earnings dashboards with sensitive info redacted)

Questions to Ask References:

  • "How much has your income increased since partnering with this agency?"
  • "How responsive is the team? Do they deliver what they promise?"
  • "Have you experienced any issues? How were they resolved?"
  • "Would you recommend this agency? Why or why not?"
  • "What's one thing you wish you'd known before signing?"

Red Flag: Agency refuses to provide references or only offers generic anonymous testimonials

8. Content Ownership & Intellectual Property Rights

What to Look For:

  • You retain 100% ownership of all content you create
  • Clear terms on what agency can use content for (promotion, portfolio, etc.)
  • Content usage rights terminate when contract ends
  • No perpetual licensing of your content to third parties

Red Flags:

  • Agency claims ownership or licensing rights to your content
  • Contract grants them ability to sell/redistribute your content
  • Post-termination content usage rights (they can keep using after you leave)
  • Vague language about "shared IP" or "collaborative content ownership"

Non-Negotiable Clause: "Creator retains full ownership of all content. Agency may use content solely for Creator's OnlyFans promotion during contract term. All rights revert to Creator upon termination."

9. Payment Terms & Payout Schedule

What to Look For:

  • Payments come directly from OnlyFans to YOUR bank account (not through agency)
  • Agency invoices you for their commission separately (or you pay them their %)
  • Clear payment schedule (e.g., "Creator pays agency commission within 5 days of receiving OnlyFans payout")
  • Late payment terms that are reasonable

Red Flags:

  • OnlyFans payouts go to agency first, then they "pay you your share"
  • Delays in commission calculations or unclear accounting
  • Excessive late fees or penalty clauses
  • Agency controls your payout account

Best Practice: You receive 100% from OnlyFans, then pay agency their commission. This ensures you always have cash flow control.

10. Non-Compete & Exclusivity Clauses

What to Look For:

  • Reasonable exclusivity during contract term (you can't work with competing agencies simultaneously)
  • Post-termination non-compete limited to 30-90 days maximum
  • Non-compete restricted to adult content management only (doesn't prevent you from other work)
  • Allowance for you to maintain existing brand partnerships/sponsorships

Red Flags:

  • Post-termination non-compete longer than 90 days
  • Restrictions on working with ANY agency or manager in the future
  • Prohibitions on creating content independently after contract ends
  • Geographic restrictions (e.g., "Creator cannot work with any US-based agency for 2 years")

Negotiation Tip: Push back on post-termination restrictions. If they're confident in their service, they shouldn't need to restrict your future opportunities.

11. Liability, Indemnification & Legal Protections

What to Look For:

  • Clear allocation of liability (who's responsible if something goes wrong)
  • Agency carries appropriate business insurance
  • Mutual indemnification (both parties protect each other from claims arising from their actions)
  • Limitation of liability clauses that are balanced (not one-sided)

Key Questions:

  • "What happens if your chatters violate OnlyFans TOS and my account gets banned?"
  • "Who's liable if marketing activities result in copyright claims?"
  • "Do you carry errors & omissions insurance?"
  • "What recourse do I have if services aren't delivered as promised?"

Red Flag: Contract heavily protects agency while placing all risk on creator (one-sided indemnification, broad liability waivers)

12. Data Privacy & Subscriber Information

What to Look For:

  • Clear data privacy terms (how subscriber data is used, stored, protected)
  • Prohibition on agency using subscriber data for other purposes
  • Data security measures (encryption, secure access protocols)
  • Return/deletion of subscriber data upon contract termination

Critical Protections:

  • Agency cannot sell or share subscriber data with third parties
  • Agency cannot contact your subscribers for other agencies/creators
  • All data must be deleted within 30 days of contract termination
  • Compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations

Red Flag: Vague data terms or language granting agency broad rights to "use subscriber information for business purposes"

13. Communication Protocols & Transparency

What to Look For:

  • Assigned point of contact with guaranteed response time (24-48 hours)
  • Regular check-in meetings (weekly or bi-weekly during onboarding, monthly ongoing)
  • Transparent access to analytics and performance data
  • Clear escalation process if issues arise

Questions to Ask:

  • "Who will be my main contact? Can I meet them before signing?"
  • "How quickly do you respond to creator questions?"
  • "What if I'm unhappy with chatting quality or marketing results? What's the process?"
  • "Will I have real-time access to see what you're doing on my account?"

Red Flag: No assigned point of contact, slow response times during sales process, vague answers about communication structure

14. Upfront Costs & Financial Investment

What to Look For:

  • Zero upfront fees (or nominal setup fee under $200)
  • Performance-based commission structure only
  • No required minimum revenue commitments
  • Agency makes money when you make money (aligned incentives)

Red Flags:

  • Upfront "setup fees" of $500+ (especially $2,000-$5,000)
  • Required purchase of "proprietary software" or "creator tools"
  • Monthly retainer fees regardless of performance
  • Requirement to use specific (expensive) photographers, content creation services, etc.

The Rule: Legitimate agencies earn through performance commission. If they're demanding thousands upfront, they're running a scam.

15. Legal Review & Negotiation Flexibility

What to Look For:

  • Agency encourages you to have contract reviewed by a lawyer
  • Willingness to negotiate terms and answer legal questions
  • Flexibility on specific clauses you're uncomfortable with
  • Professional, patient approach to contract discussions

Red Flags:

  • "This is our standard contract, we can't change anything"
  • Pressure to sign immediately without legal review
  • Defensive or aggressive responses to questions about terms
  • Artificial urgency ("sign by Friday or we give your spot to someone else")

Non-Negotiable: ALWAYS have an attorney review the contract before signing. Spend $300-$500 on legal review to avoid $50k+ in future losses.

Sample Questions to Ask During Agency Interviews

About Their Business

  • "How long have you been managing OnlyFans creators?"
  • "How many creators do you currently manage?"
  • "What's your average client revenue increase in the first 90 days?"
  • "Can you show me your business registration and any relevant certifications?"

About Services

  • "Walk me through a typical week of what you'd do for my account."
  • "How many chatters would work on my account? Can I meet them?"
  • "What marketing channels do you actively use for promotion?"
  • "How do you handle content strategy and pricing optimization?"

About Results

  • "Can you provide 5 references from current creators I can speak with?"
  • "What's your creator retention rate? How many creators renew contracts?"
  • "What happens if my earnings don't increase? Is there a performance guarantee?"
  • "Can you show me real case studies with verifiable before/after metrics?"

About The Relationship

  • "How often will we communicate? Who's my main point of contact?"
  • "What oversight and approval do I have over chatting and marketing?"
  • "How do I terminate the contract if I'm unhappy? What would it cost?"
  • "Do you have other creators in my specific niche? How do you prevent conflicts?"

The Pre-Signing Legal Review Checklist

Before you sign, ensure your attorney (or you) reviews these specific sections:

  • ☐ Commission calculation methodology (get examples in writing)
  • ☐ Contract duration and renewal terms
  • ☐ Termination clauses and exit costs
  • ☐ Scope of services with specific deliverables
  • ☐ Account access and security provisions
  • ☐ Content ownership and IP rights
  • ☐ Data privacy and subscriber information handling
  • ☐ Liability and indemnification terms
  • ☐ Non-compete and exclusivity restrictions
  • ☐ Payment terms and payout schedule
  • ☐ Dispute resolution process
  • ☐ Amendment procedures (how changes to contract are made)
  • ☐ Force majeure and unforeseen circumstances
  • ☐ Confidentiality obligations (both ways)
  • ☐ Governing law and jurisdiction

Final Thoughts: Trust, But Verify

The right agency partnership can be transformational. The wrong one can be devastating.

Don't let excitement, urgency, or persuasive sales tactics rush you into signing a contract you don't fully understand. This is a business decision worth tens or hundreds of thousands of dollars—treat it accordingly.

Key Principles:

  • If you don't understand something in the contract, don't sign until you do
  • If they pressure you to sign quickly, that's a red flag—walk away
  • If they won't provide references or case studies, they're hiding something
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it is
  • Always get legal review for contracts worth $10k+ annually

Use this checklist as your shield against predatory agencies and your compass toward legitimate partnerships that truly accelerate your OnlyFans success.
