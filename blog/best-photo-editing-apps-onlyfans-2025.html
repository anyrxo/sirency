Essential Editing Tips for OnlyFans Content

1. Less is More

The best editing is invisible. Over-smoothed skin, over-saturated colors, and heavy filters look fake. Aim for "enhanced natural" not "obviously edited."

2. Establish Presets for Consistency

Create or find presets that match your brand. Use the same preset/filter across content for cohesive aesthetic. Subscribers notice consistency.

3. Edit in Good Lighting

Edit photos in bright, neutral lighting. Colors look different on your phone at night vs. daytime, leading to inconsistent editing.

4. Save Originals

Always keep unedited versions. Editing trends change, and you may want to re-edit old photos with new techniques.

5. Batch Edit Similar Photos

When editing photos from the same shoot, use the same settings for all. This creates consistency and speeds up workflow.
