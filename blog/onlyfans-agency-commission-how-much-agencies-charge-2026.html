üîç Hidden Commission Costs to Watch For

The stated commission isn't always the full cost. Watch for these hidden fees that inflate actual costs:

üö© Hidden Fee Red Flag #1: Software/Tool Fees

The scam: "30% commission + $300/month software fee for analytics tools"

Why it's unfair: Legitimate agencies include all software costs in commission. Charging separately is double-dipping. If you're earning $10K monthly, $300 fee = effective 33% commission, not 30%.

Fair alternative: All tools, software, and resources included in stated commission percentage with no additional fees.

üö© Hidden Fee Red Flag #2: "Marketing Budget" Requirements

The scam: "30% commission, but you must spend minimum $1,000/month on paid ads we manage"

Why it's unfair: Unless the agency GUARANTEES 3-5x ROI on ad spend with refund policy, mandatory ad budgets are risky. Some agencies push ads to inflate apparent "results" while you fund it.

Fair alternative: Paid advertising optional, only recommended when you're earning $10K+ monthly, with transparent ROI tracking showing every dollar's return.

üö© Hidden Fee Red Flag #3: Setup/Onboarding Fees

The scam: "30% commission + $1,500 one-time setup fee"

Why it's suspicious: Most legitimate agencies include onboarding in commission. Setup fees can be cash grabs, especially if they're non-refundable and agency disappears after payment.

Fair alternative: No upfront fees. Small refundable deposits ($100-$300 applied to first month) are borderline acceptable, but $1,000+ setup fees = red flag.

üö© Hidden Fee Red Flag #4: "Success Fees" or Performance Bonuses

The scam: "25% base commission + 10% performance bonus if you exceed $20K monthly"

Why it's complicated: This effectively becomes 35% commission at higher earnings‚Äîexactly when you need commission to decrease, not increase. Penalizes your success.

Fair alternative: Flat commission regardless of earnings level, or TIERED DISCOUNTS (30% for first $10K, 25% for earnings above $10K).
