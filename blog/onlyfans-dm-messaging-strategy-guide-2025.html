Understanding Message Types

OnlyFans offers three primary messaging approaches, each with distinct purposes:

1. Mass Messages (Broadcast)

What they are: Messages sent simultaneously to all subscribers or specific segments

Best for:

  • PPV content sales to large audiences
  • Announcements and updates
  • Limited-time promotional offers
  • Holiday greetings and seasonal content

Frequency: 2-3 times weekly is optimal. More than 5 weekly risks annoying subscribers.

Typical unlock rate: 15-25% for well-crafted messages

2. One-on-One Messages

What they are: Individual personalized conversations with specific subscribers

Best for:

  • High-value VIP subscribers (top 20% of spenders)
  • Custom content requests and negotiations
  • Building deeper relationships with loyal fans
  • Addressing specific questions or concerns

Time investment: Focus on subscribers who spend $100+ monthly

Response time target: Within 2-4 hours during active hours

3. Automated Messages

What they are: Pre-programmed messages triggered by subscriber actions

Best for:

  • Welcome sequences for new subscribers
  • Re-engagement campaigns for inactive fans
  • 24/7 responses when you're offline
  • Scaling messaging beyond manual capacity

Common Messaging Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Sending Too Many Mass Messages

The Problem: Messaging daily or multiple times per day overwhelms subscribers.

Why It Hurts: Subscribers mute notifications, block messages, or cancel entirely. You train them to ignore your messages.

Fix: Stick to 2-3 mass messages per week maximum. Quality over quantity. Make each message count.

Mistake 2: Generic, Impersonal Messages

The Problem: "Hey guys!" or "What's up everyone!" in mass messages.

Why It Hurts: Feels spam-like and transactional. No personal connection.

Fix: Use "you" language. "I made this for you..." instead of "I made new content for everyone!"

Mistake 3: No Preview Images

The Problem: Sending PPV messages with only text description and locked content.

Why It Hurts: Unlock rates drop 40-60% without previews. Subscribers don't buy blind.

Fix: Always include 1-3 teaser images showing what they'll unlock. Enough to entice, not enough to satisfy.

Mistake 4: Vague or Weak CTAs

The Problem: "Let me know if you want this" or "Available if interested."

Why It Hurts: Weak CTAs don't drive action. Subscribers scroll past.

Fix: Direct, specific CTAs: "Unlock below to watch now!" or "Tap to get yours before it expires!"

Mistake 5: Ignoring Response Time

The Problem: Taking 12-24+ hours to respond to interested subscribers.

Why It Hurts: Momentum dies. Subscriber loses interest or spends money elsewhere.

Fix: Respond to purchase inquiries and custom requests within 2-4 hours maximum. Use automation for off-hours.

Mistake 6: Never Asking for Feedback

The Problem: Only sending sales messages, never engaging conversationally.

Why It Hurts: Feels one-sided and transactional. Subscribers don't feel valued.

Fix: Monthly "How am I doing?" message. Ask what they want to see more/less of. Shows you care.
