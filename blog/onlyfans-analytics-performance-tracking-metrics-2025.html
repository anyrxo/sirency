The Core Metrics Every Creator Must Track

Define 2-5 key performance indicators (KPIs) aligned to your goals. Consistently tracking these vital few KPIs will provide focus and gauge your progress. Here are the essential metrics for OnlyFans success:

1. Revenue Metrics

Total Monthly Revenue (TMR)

Your total earnings from all sources combined.

TMR = Subscription Revenue + PPV Sales + Tips + Custom Content + Other Income

Benchmark Targets by Growth Stage:

  • Beginner (0-6 months): $500-2,000/month
  • Growing (6-12 months): $2,000-10,000/month
  • Established (1-2 years): $10,000-30,000/month
  • Elite (Top 1%): $30,000-500,000+/month

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

How much each subscriber generates on average per month.

ARPU = Total Monthly Revenue √∑ Total Active Subscribers

2025 ARPU Benchmarks:

  • Low ARPU: $5-15/subscriber/month
  • Average ARPU: $15-30/subscriber/month
  • High ARPU: $30-60/subscriber/month
  • Elite ARPU: $60-150+/subscriber/month

Revenue Mix Percentage

Breakdown of revenue sources to identify diversification and opportunities.

2025 Industry Standard:

  • Subscription Revenue: 42% of total (down from 61% in 2023)
  • PPV Messages: 25-35% of total
  • Tips: 10-15% of total
  • Custom Content: 8-12% of total
  • Other (bundles, locked posts): 5-10% of total

Insight: Diversified revenue sources indicate business health and resilience against subscription churn.

2. Subscriber Metrics

Total Active Subscribers

Number of currently subscribed, paying members.

Net New Subscribers (NNS)

New subscribers gained minus subscribers lost in a period.

NNS = New Subscribers - Cancelled Subscribers

Growth Rate

Percentage growth in subscriber base month-over-month.

Growth Rate = ((Current Month Subs - Last Month Subs) √∑ Last Month Subs) √ó 100

Healthy Growth Rate Targets:

  • Beginner Stage: 20-50% monthly growth
  • Growth Stage: 10-25% monthly growth
  • Maturity Stage: 5-15% monthly growth

Subscriber Lifetime Value (LTV)

Total revenue expected from a subscriber over their entire relationship with you.

LTV = ARPU √ó Average Subscriber Lifespan (in months)

Example: ARPU of $40/month √ó 7.3 month average lifespan = $292 LTV per subscriber

3. Retention & Churn Metrics

Retention Rate

Percentage of subscribers who renew/remain subscribed over time.

Retention Rate = (Subscribers at End of Period √∑ Subscribers at Start of Period) √ó 100

2025 Retention Benchmarks:

  • First Month Renewal: 60% industry average
  • 3-Month Retention: 40-50% (good), 60-70% (excellent)
  • 6-Month Retention: 25-35% (good), 50-60% (excellent)
  • 12-Month Retention: 15-20% (good), 35-45% (excellent)

Churn Rate

Percentage of subscribers who cancel within a 30-day period.

Churn Rate = (Cancelled Subscribers √∑ Total Subscribers at Start of Month) √ó 100

Churn Rate Benchmarks:

  • Average Creator: 40-60% monthly churn
  • Good Performer: 20-40% monthly churn
  • Top Performer: Below 4% monthly churn (elite tier)

2025 Data Insight: One top-5 creator reported churn fell below 4% by implementing structured communication strategies and consistent posting (15+ posts/month).

Average Subscriber Lifespan

How long, on average, a subscriber stays before cancelling.

Avg Lifespan = 1 √∑ Monthly Churn Rate

2025 Industry Data:

  • Overall Average: 4.7 months per subscriber
  • Consistent Posters (15+ posts/month): 7.3 months per subscriber
  • Elite Creators (top 1%): 10-18 months per subscriber

3. Fan Segmentation Analysis

Categorize subscribers by spending behavior and engagement level.

Fan Segment Categories:

  • Whales (Top 10% Spenders): Generate 60-80% of total revenue
  • Engaged Fans (Active, Mid-Spend): Regular engagement, moderate PPV purchases
  • Lurkers (Low Engagement): Subscribed but minimal interaction
  • At-Risk (Low Activity): Not opening messages, candidates for churn

Tailored Strategies by Segment:

  • Whales: VIP treatment, exclusive content, personal thank-yous, priority DMs
  • Engaged Fans: Regular PPV offers, community building, bundle promotions
  • Lurkers: Re-engagement campaigns, exclusive teasers, special offers
  • At-Risk: Win-back campaigns, limited-time discounts, direct outreach

4. A/B Testing & Experimentation

Test variables to optimize performance based on data, not assumptions.

What to A/B Test:

  • Subscription Price: $9.99 vs $14.99 vs $19.99
  • PPV Pricing: $15 vs $20 vs $25 for same content type
  • Message Timing: Morning vs evening PPV sends
  • Content Thumbnails: Different preview images for same post
  • Bio Copy: Different value propositions in profile description
  • Free Trial Duration: 3-day vs 7-day vs 30-day trials
  • Posting Frequency: 2 posts/day vs 3 posts/day vs 5 posts/day

A/B Testing Protocol:

  1. Define hypothesis (e.g., "Increasing PPV price to $25 will increase total revenue despite lower purchase rate")
  2. Test with 50% of audience for 2 weeks minimum
  3. Measure key metrics (revenue, conversion rate, engagement)
  4. Analyze results for statistical significance
  5. Implement winning variation for all subscribers
